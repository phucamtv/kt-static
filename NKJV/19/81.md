<div class="title"><h1>81</h1><h3>An Appeal for Israel’s Repentance</h3><i>To the Chief Musician. <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ps. 8:title">⚓</a>On an instrument of Gath. A Psalm of Asaph.</i></div>
<span class="verse thi_81_1">  <sup>1</sup>Sing aloud to God our strength;<br/>   Make a joyful shout to the God of Jacob.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_2">   <sup>2</sup>Raise a song and strike the timbrel,<br/>   The pleasant harp with the lute.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_3">  <sup>3</sup>Blow the trumpet at the time of the New Moon,<br/>   At the full moon, on our solemn feast day.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_4">   <sup>4</sup>For <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Lev. 23:24; Num. 10:10">⚓</a>this <i>is</i> a statute for Israel,<br/>   A law of the God of Jacob.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_5">   <sup>5</sup>This He established in Joseph <i>as</i> a testimony,<br/>   When He went throughout the land of Egypt,<br/>   <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Deut. 28:49; Ps. 114:1; Jer. 5:15">⚓</a><i>Where</i> I heard a language I did not understand.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_6">  <sup>6</sup>“I removed his shoulder from the burden;<br/>   His hands were freed from the baskets.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_7">   <sup>7</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ex. 2:23; 14:10; Ps. 50:15">⚓</a>You called in trouble, and I delivered you;<br/>   <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ex. 19:19; 20:18">⚓</a>I answered you in the secret place of thunder;<br/>   I <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ex. 17:6, 7; Num. 20:13">⚓</a>tested you at the waters of Meribah.<br/> <i>Selah</i><br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_8">  <sup>8</sup>“Hear,<a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Ps. 50:7)">⚓</a> O My people, and I will admonish you!<br/>   O Israel, if you will listen to Me!<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_9">   <sup>9</sup>There shall be no <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Ex. 20:3; Deut. 5:7; 32:12); Ps. 44:20; (Is. 43:12)">⚓</a>foreign god among you;<br/>   Nor shall you worship any foreign god.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_10">   <sup>10</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ex. 20:2; Deut. 5:6">⚓</a>I <i>am</i> the Lord your God,<br/>   Who brought you out of the land of Egypt;<br/>   <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ps. 103:5">⚓</a>Open your mouth wide, and I will fill it.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_11">  <sup>11</sup>“But My people would not heed My voice,<br/>   And Israel would <i>have</i><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ex. 32:1; Deut. 32:15">⚓</a>none of Me.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_12">   <sup>12</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Job 8:4; Acts 7:42; Rom. 1:24, 26)">⚓</a>So I gave them over to their own stubborn heart,<br/>   To walk in their own counsels.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_13">  <sup>13</sup>“Oh,<a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Deut. 5:29; Is. 48:18)">⚓</a> that My people would listen to Me,<br/>   That Israel would walk in My ways!<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_14">   <sup>14</sup>I would soon subdue their enemies,<br/>   And turn My hand against their adversaries.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_15">   <sup>15</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Rom. 1:30">⚓</a>The haters of the Lord would pretend submission to Him,<br/>   But their fate would endure forever.<br/></span>
<span class="verse thi_81_16">   <sup>16</sup>He would <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Deut. 32:14">⚓</a>have fed them also with the finest of wheat;<br/>   And with honey <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Job 29:6">⚓</a>from the rock I would have satisfied you.”<br/></span>
