<div class="title"><h1>17</h1></div>
<span class="verse ch_17_1">  <sup>1</sup>Better <i>is</i><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 15:17">⚓</a>a dry morsel with quietness,<br/>   Than a house full of feasting <i>with</i> strife.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_2">  <sup>2</sup>A wise servant will rule over <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 10:5">⚓</a>a son who causes shame,<br/>   And will share an inheritance among the brothers.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_3">  <sup>3</sup>The refining pot <i>is</i> for silver and the furnace for gold,<br/>   <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="1 Chr. 29:17; Ps. 26:2; Prov. 15:11; Jer. 17:10; (Mal. 3:3)">⚓</a>But the Lord tests the hearts.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_4">  <sup>4</sup>An evildoer gives heed to false lips;<br/>   A liar listens eagerly to a spiteful tongue.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_5">  <sup>5</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 14:31">⚓</a>He who mocks the poor reproaches his Maker;<br/>   <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Job 31:29; Prov. 24:17; Obad. 12; 1 Cor. 13:6">⚓</a>He who is glad at calamity will not go unpunished.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_6">  <sup>6</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Ps. 127:3; 128:3)">⚓</a>Children’s children <i>are</i> the crown of old men,<br/>   And the glory of children <i>is</i> their father.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_7">   <sup>7</sup>Excellent speech is not becoming to a fool,<br/>   Much less lying lips to a prince.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_8">  <sup>8</sup>A present <i>is</i> a precious stone in the eyes of its possessor;<br/>   Wherever he turns, he prospers.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_9">  <sup>9</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Prov. 10:12; 1 Cor. 13:5–7; James 5:20)">⚓</a>He who covers a transgression seeks love,<br/>   But <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 16:28">⚓</a>he who repeats a matter separates friends.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_10">  <sup>10</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 10:17; (Mic. 7:9)">⚓</a>Rebuke is more effective for a wise <i>man</i><br/>   Than a hundred blows on a fool.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_11">  <sup>11</sup>An evil <i>man</i> seeks only rebellion;<br/>   Therefore a cruel messenger will be sent against him.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_12">  <sup>12</sup>Let a man meet <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="2 Sam. 17:8; Hos. 13:8">⚓</a>a bear robbed of her cubs,<br/>   Rather than a fool in his folly.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_13">  <sup>13</sup>Whoever <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ps. 109:4, 5; Jer. 18:20; Rom. 12:17; 1 Thess. 5:15; (1 Pet. 3:9)">⚓</a>rewards evil for good,<br/>   Evil will not depart from his house.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_14">  <sup>14</sup>The beginning of strife <i>is</i> <i>like</i> releasing water;<br/>   Therefore <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Prov. 20:3; 1 Thess. 4:11)">⚓</a>stop contention before a quarrel starts.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_15">  <sup>15</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ex. 23:7; Prov. 24:24; Is. 5:23">⚓</a>He who justifies the wicked, and he who condemns the just,<br/>   Both of them alike <i>are</i> an abomination to the Lord.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_16">  <sup>16</sup>Why <i>is</i> <i>there</i> in the hand of a fool the purchase price of wisdom,<br/>   Since <i>he</i> <i>has</i> no heart <i>for</i> <i>it?</i><br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_17">  <sup>17</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ruth 1:16; Prov. 18:24">⚓</a>A friend loves at all times,<br/>   And a brother is born for adversity.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_18">  <sup>18</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 6:1">⚓</a>A man devoid of understanding shakes hands in a pledge,<br/>   <i>And</i> becomes surety for his friend.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_19">  <sup>19</sup>He who loves transgression loves strife,<br/>   And <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 16:18">⚓</a>he who exalts his gate seeks destruction.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_20">  <sup>20</sup>He who has a deceitful heart finds no good,<br/>   And he who has <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="James 3:8">⚓</a>a perverse tongue falls into evil.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_21">  <sup>21</sup>He who begets a scoffer <i>does</i> <i>so</i> to his sorrow,<br/>   And the father of a fool has no joy.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_22">  <sup>22</sup>A <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 12:25; 15:13, 15">⚓</a>merry heart does good, <i>like</i> medicine,<br/>   But a broken spirit dries the bones.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_23">  <sup>23</sup>A wicked <i>man</i> accepts a bribe behind the back<br/>   To pervert the ways of justice.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_24">  <sup>24</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Eccl. 2:14">⚓</a>Wisdom <i>is</i> in the sight of him who has understanding,<br/>   But the eyes of a fool <i>are</i> on the ends of the earth.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_25">  <sup>25</sup>A <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 10:1; 15:20; 19:13">⚓</a>foolish son <i>is</i> a grief to his father,<br/>   And bitterness to her who bore him.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_26">  <sup>26</sup>Also, to punish the righteous <i>is</i> not good,<br/>   <i>Nor</i> to strike princes for <i>their</i> uprightness.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_27">  <sup>27</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 10:19; James 1:19">⚓</a>He who has knowledge spares his words,<br/>   <i>And</i> a man of understanding is of a calm spirit.<br/></span>
<span class="verse ch_17_28">  <sup>28</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Job 13:5">⚓</a>Even a fool is counted wise when he holds his peace;<br/>   <i>When</i> he shuts his lips, <i>he</i> <i>is</i> <i>considered</i> perceptive.<br/></span>
