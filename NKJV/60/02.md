<div class="title"><h1>2</h1></div>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_1"> <sup>1</sup>Therefore, <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Heb. 12:1">⚓</a>laying aside all malice, all deceit, hypocrisy, envy, and all evil speaking, </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_2"><sup>2</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Matt. 18:3; 19:14; Mark 10:15; Luke 18:17); 1 Cor. 14:20">⚓</a>as newborn babes, desire the pure <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="1 Cor. 3:2">⚓</a>milk of the word, that you may grow thereby, </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_3"><sup>3</sup>if indeed you have <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ps. 34:8; Titus 3:4; Heb. 6:5">⚓</a>tasted that the Lord <i>is</i> gracious.<br/></span>
<div class="title"><h3>The Chosen Stone and His Chosen People</h3></div>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_4"> <sup>4</sup>Coming to Him <i>as</i> <i>to</i> a living stone, <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ps. 118:22">⚓</a>rejected indeed by men, but chosen by God <i>and</i> precious, </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_5"><sup>5</sup>you also, as living stones, are being built up a spiritual house, a holy priesthood, to offer up spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God through Jesus Christ. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_6"><sup>6</sup>Therefore it is also contained in the Scripture,<br/>  <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 28:16; Rom. 9:32, 33; 10:11; 1 Pet. 2:8">⚓</a>“Behold, I lay in Zion<br/>   A chief cornerstone, elect, precious,<br/>   And he who believes on Him will by no means be put to shame.”<br/></span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_7"> <sup>7</sup>Therefore, to you who believe, <i>He</i> <i>is</i> precious; but to those who are disobedient,<br/>  <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Ps. 118:22; Matt. 21:42; Luke 2:34">⚓</a>“The stone which the builders rejected<br/>   Has become the chief cornerstone,”<br/></span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_8"> <sup>8</sup>and<br/>  <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 8:14">⚓</a>“A stone of stumbling<br/>   And a rock of offense.”<br/> <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="1 Cor. 1:23; Gal. 5:11">⚓</a>They stumble, being disobedient to the word, <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Rom. 9:22">⚓</a>to which they also were appointed.<br/></span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_9"> <sup>9</sup>But you <i>are</i> a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, His own special people, that you may proclaim the praises of Him who called you out of <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 9:2; 42:16; (Acts 26:18; 2 Cor. 4:6)">⚓</a>darkness into His marvelous light; </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_10"><sup>10</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Hos. 1:9, 10; 2:23; Rom. 9:25; 10:19">⚓</a>who once <i>were</i> not a people but <i>are</i> now the people of God, who had not obtained mercy but now have obtained mercy.<br/></span>
<div class="title"><h3>Living Before the World</h3></div>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_11"> <sup>11</sup>Beloved, I beg <i>you</i> as sojourners and pilgrims, abstain from fleshly lusts <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(Rom. 8:13); Gal. 5:17; James 4:1">⚓</a>which war against the soul, </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_12"><sup>12</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="2 Cor. 8:21; Phil. 2:15; Titus 2:8; 1 Pet. 2:15; 3:16">⚓</a>having your conduct honorable among the Gentiles, that when they speak against you as evildoers, <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Matt. 5:16; 9:8; John 13:31; 1 Pet. 4:11, 16">⚓</a>they may, by <i>your</i> good works which they observe, glorify God in the day of visitation.<br/></span>
<div class="title"><h3>Submission to Government</h3></div>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_13"> <sup>13</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Matt. 22:21">⚓</a>Therefore submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord’s sake, whether to the king as supreme, </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_14"><sup>14</sup>or to governors, as to those who are sent by him for the punishment of evildoers and <i>for</i> <i>the</i> praise of those who do good. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_15"><sup>15</sup>For this is the will of God, that by doing good you may put to silence the ignorance of foolish men— </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_16"><sup>16</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Rom. 6:14, 20, 22; 1 Cor. 7:22; (Gal. 5:1)">⚓</a>as free, yet not <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Gal. 5:13">⚓</a>using liberty as a cloak for vice, but as bondservants of God. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_17"><sup>17</sup>Honor all <i>people.</i> Love the brotherhood. Fear <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Prov. 24:21">⚓</a>God. Honor the king.<br/></span>
<div class="title"><h3>Submission to Masters</h3></div>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_18"> <sup>18</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Eph. 6:5–8">⚓</a>Servants, <i>be</i> submissive to <i>your</i> masters with all fear, not only to the good and gentle, but also to the harsh. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_19"><sup>19</sup>For this <i>is</i><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Matt. 5:10">⚓</a>commendable, if because of conscience toward God one endures grief, suffering wrongfully. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_20"><sup>20</sup>For <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Luke 6:32–34">⚓</a>what credit <i>is</i> <i>it</i> if, when you are beaten for your faults, you take it patiently? But when you do good and suffer, if you take it patiently, this <i>is</i> commendable before God. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_21"><sup>21</sup>For <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Matt. 16:24; 1 Thess. 3:3, 4">⚓</a>to this you were called, because Christ also suffered for us, <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="(1 John 2:6)">⚓</a>leaving us an example, that you should follow His steps:<br/></span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_22">  <sup>22</sup>“Who <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 53:9; 2 Cor. 5:21">⚓</a>committed no sin,<br/>   Nor was deceit found in His mouth”; <br/></span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_23"> <sup>23</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 53:7; Heb. 12:3; 1 Pet. 3:9">⚓</a>who, when He was reviled, did not revile in return; when He suffered, He did not threaten, but <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Luke 23:46">⚓</a>committed <i>Himself</i> to Him who judges righteously; </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_24"><sup>24</sup><a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 53:4, 11; 1 Cor. 15:3; (Heb. 9:28)">⚓</a>who Himself bore our sins in His own body on the tree, <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Rom. 7:6">⚓</a>that we, having died to sins, might live for righteousness—<a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 53:5">⚓</a>by whose stripes you were healed. </span>
<span class="verse 1phi_2_25"><sup>25</sup>For <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 53:5, 6">⚓</a>you were like sheep going astray, but have now returned <a data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="bottom" title="Is. 40:11; (Ezek. 34:23); Zech. 13:7">⚓</a>to the Shepherd and Overseer of your souls.<br/></span>
